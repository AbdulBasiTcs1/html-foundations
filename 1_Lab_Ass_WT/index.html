Hardware in Focus

A visual tour of core computer components

CPU

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The CPU is the brain of the computer. It executes instructions, performs calculations, and manages data flow between all components. Modern CPUs contain billions of transistors and run at speeds measured in gigahertz (GHz).

GPU

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

The GPU is specialized hardware designed to render images, video, and animations. Originally for graphics, GPUs are now also used in AI, scientific simulations, and data processing due to their massive parallel processing power.

Input Devices

Input devices allow users to send data and commands to the computer. Common input devices include keyboards, mice, scanners, microphones, webcams, and touchscreens. They convert human actions into digital signals.

Output Devices

Output devices display or produce the results of computer processing. Monitors, printers, speakers, and projectors are all output devices. They translate digital data back into human-readable forms such as text, images, or audio.

About Us

TechCore Hub is an academic project developed by CS students at COMSATS University Islamabad, Attock Campus as part of the Web Technology course (Spring 2026). Our mission is to build a comprehensive, free, and easy-to-understand educational platform dedicated to computer hardware.

We believe every student deserves access to quality learning material. By combining clear explanations, visual diagrams, and real-world examples, TechCore Hub makes hardware concepts — that can feel intimidating — approachable and even exciting.

From the fundamentals of binary logic to the inner workings of a modern CPU, our content is structured to guide beginners and strengthen the knowledge of advanced learners alike.
